What is the IB?

The International Baccalaureate (IB) is the world's most respected international education program, recognized by top universities in over 140 countries. IB graduates are known for being independent thinkers, strong writers, and confident in more than one language. Exactly the profile universities and employers look for. Westhill is one of only 4 schools in Mexico City that offers the complete IB program from preschool through high school.

Section 02 - The IB Continuum at Westhill

One philosophy. Twelve years. PreK to graduation.

What the IB continuum gives your child

From ages 3 through 18, your child follows one connected learning philosophy, not a patchwork of programs. Critical thinking habits start in PreK, deepen in middle school, and culminate in a globally recognized high school diploma. No transition shock between sections. No starting over. Just one continuous, increasingly rigorous education designed by the IB for international students.

PYP
Primary Years Programme
Ages 3–11 · PreK to Grade 5

Students learn through curiosity and inquiry, asking questions and connecting ideas across subjects. Builds the foundation of being a confident, independent thinker.

MYP
Middle Years Programme
Ages 11–16 · Grades 6 to 10

Students take 8 subject areas and learn to apply them to real-world challenges. Develops strong writing, research, and the discipline needed for the Diploma Programme.

DP

Ages 16–18 · Grades 11 and 12

The globally recognized diploma accepted by top universities in 140+ countries. Includes a major research essay and an independent project. Students arrive at university already trained for it.

The NWEA MAP Growth assessment is the world's most widely used international benchmark for student learning, taken by over 13 million students across 145 countries. It compares your child's performance to a global average, not just other Mexican schools. Westhill High School students consistently score above that global average in every subject tested.
